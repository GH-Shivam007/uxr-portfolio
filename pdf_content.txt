Demystifying Notification Fatigue 
 A mixed-methods diary study on user behavior towards frequent notifications 
~ Shivam Jha 

Problem 
Users are overwhelmed by notifications and often ignore or dismiss them, but 
the reasons behind these behaviors are not well understood. 
Goal 
To identify what drives users to open, ignore, or dismiss notifications and 
understand how importance and context influence these decisions. 
A diary-based study using Google Forms, followed by data cleaning, 
tagging, and pivot-based analysis to uncover behavioral patterns. 
 Methodology

Systemic Fixes for Attention 
Architecture 
Context-First Payloads 
Strict Relevance Batching 
Granular Controls Push critical/user-triggered alerts in real-time.  Bundle algorithmic or low-value updates into silent, 
scheduled "Daily Digests." 
Inject mandatory "Reason for Alert"  metadata into subtitles (e.g., "Action Required") to 
eliminate ambiguity and anxiety clicks. 
Replace binary system-level On/Off switches with user-defined preference logic, allowing users to 
build their own relevance filters.
